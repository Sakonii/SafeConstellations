\begin{abstract}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------

Representation steering suppresses safety refusals in aligned language models by ablating
a ``refusal direction'' extracted from harmful/harmless contrastive pairs---but does it
address \emph{over-refusal} (refusing safe, task-wrapped instructions) selectively?
We investigate this through directional geometry analysis, constellation mapping, and
causal circuit attribution on LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct.
We find that harmful-refusal and over-refusal directions are geometrically distinct:
$\cos(\hat{\mathbf{v}}^{\text{OR}}, \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}) \approx 0.45$ at
L12, roughly half the 0.85 alignment found among per-task harmful-refusal directions.
Per-task over-refusal directions are also more task-specific (pairwise cosine $0.56$
vs.\ $0.845$ for harmful-refusal). The OR subspace is higher-dimensional: 11 principal
components are needed to capture 80\% of its variance at L12 (vs.\ 8 for
harmful-refusal), and its principal axes are task-identity directions.
Mid-layer activations are dominated by \emph{task-identity constellations}
(silhouette\,=\,0.357 at L12) within which over-refusal is a small within-cluster perturbation.
Crucially, while Arditi ablation does suppress both harmful-refusal ($-55$ pp) and
over-refusal ($-50$ pp), it does so \emph{non-selectively}---using a blunt
instrument that disrupts the entire refusal circuit regardless of cause.
A direct head-to-head experiment confirms that SafeConstellations is more selective:
it reduces over-refusal to 0\% ($-55$ pp) while causing less harmful-refusal bypass
($-35$ pp vs.\ Arditi's $-55$ pp), yielding a selectivity ratio of \textbf{1.57}
vs.\ 0.91 for Arditi ablation.
These findings reframe the question from ``can steering fix over-refusal?''
to ``can it fix over-refusal \emph{selectively}?''---a distinction invisible
to any single-direction approach.

\end{abstract}

%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\section{Introduction}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------

Safety-aligned LLMs are trained to refuse harmful requests but frequently over-refuse
safe instructions framed in sensitive contexts \citep{safeconstellations2025}: a
translation task about historical violence, a sentiment analysis request on an
inflammatory text, or a cryptographic question phrased generically are refused at higher
rates than direct harmful prompts on the same topic. This \emph{over-refusal} creates a
usability cost that falls disproportionately on professional applications.

Representation steering---ablating a ``refusal direction'' extracted via
difference-in-means (DIM) over harmful/harmless contrastive pairs---is the dominant
inference-time fix for unwanted refusals \citep{arditi2024refusal,zou2023representation}.
If over-refusal is mediated by the same geometric object as harmful-refusal, ablating
this direction should fix it. In practice, it does not \citep{wangf2025refusal}.

\textbf{This paper provides a geometric and mechanistic account of this distinction.}
Figure~\ref{fig:galaxy} illustrates the central finding: at the peak-constellation
layer (L12), task identity---not refusal class---is the dominant spatial structure.
Over-refusal cases sit \emph{inside} their task-colored clusters. The Arditi direction,
converging before constellations form (layer 3), captures a cross-class signal
geometrically distinct from the within-task OR signal ($\cos \approx 0.45$ vs.\
$0.845$ for harmful-refusal). Our generation experiment reveals that Arditi does
suppress over-refusal ($-50$ pp), but as a side-effect of broad circuit disruption---not
targeted correction. Task-conditioned methods, by contrast, operate in the
OR-specific subspace and can fix over-refusal without this collateral cost.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item We provide the first direct geometric comparison of harmful-refusal and
  over-refusal directions across all 32 layers:
  $\cos(\hat{\mathbf{v}}^{\OR}, \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}) \approx 0.45$
  at L12, roughly half the $0.845$ alignment among per-task harmful-refusal directions.
  \item We show that per-task OR directions are more task-specific than per-task
  harmful-refusal directions (pairwise cosine $0.56$ vs.\ $0.845$), establishing
  that over-refusal is a task-conditioned phenomenon.
  \item We characterize mid-layer task-constellation geometry (silhouette\,=\,0.357 at
  L12) and show over-refusal is a small within-cluster perturbation inside it.
  \item We empirically show Arditi ablation suppresses \emph{both} classes similarly
  ($-55$ pp harmful, $-50$ pp over-refusal), reframing the problem from ``does steering
  work?'' to ``is it \emph{selective}?''---a question only task-conditioned geometry
  can answer.
  \item We confirm the selectivity prediction in a direct head-to-head experiment:
  SafeConstellations reduces over-refusal to 0\% ($-55$ pp) while causing less
  harmful-refusal bypass than Arditi ($-35$ pp vs.\ $-55$ pp), for a selectivity
  ratio of \textbf{1.57} vs.\ 0.91. The residual bypass is mechanistically explained
  by constellation geometry: harmful prompts in our set are task-wrapped and share
  task-cluster identity with benign over-refusal samples at mid-layer.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\label{sec:background}

\paragraph{Refusal localization.}
\citet{arditi2024refusal} show that a DIM direction over harmful/harmless pairs is
necessary and sufficient for refusal across 13 models. \citet{zhao2025refusal} extend
this to reasoning models. Component-level analyses identify specific attention heads
\citep{zhou2025safety} and neurons \citep{zhao2025d} as primary circuit elements.
SAE-based decompositions \citep{templeton2024scaling,goyal2025detox} provide a
feature-level picture.

\paragraph{Steering limitations.}
\citet{braun2025unreliability} identify three failure conditions: high magnitude,
distributional shift, and feature entanglement. \citet{hedstrom2025steer} show that
abstaining from steering is optimal when the model lacks stable representations.
\citet{wang2025adaptive} find that a single truthfulness vector fails across
hallucination categories. These failure modes are consistent with, but do not explain,
the specific geometric structure we document for over-refusal.

\paragraph{Task-conditioned representations.}
\citet{safeconstellations2025} show that LLMs encode task identity through stable
``constellation'' trajectory patterns and that task-conditioned steering reduces
over-refusal by up to 73\%. Our paper provides the mechanistic explanation for why
this works while global ablation fails.

\paragraph{Non-linear representations.}
\citet{engels2025nonlinear} show that some concepts (temporal sequences) are encoded
as circular manifolds, not single directions. We find a related limitation for
over-refusal: the within-task OR direction is not a stable global direction but is
task-specific and geometrically orthogonal to the harmful-refusal axis.

\section{Setup}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\label{sec:setup}

\paragraph{Model and data.}
We study \textbf{LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct} (32 layers, 4096-dim). Our evaluation set
has 270 samples across five tasks: sentiment analysis, translation, cryptanalysis,
RAG-style QA, and rephrasing. Each sample is labeled with a \emph{response label}
(\texttt{cautious / not\_harmful / harmful}) and a \emph{refusal class}
(\texttt{direct\_answer / direct\_refusal / indirect\_refusal}) via a GPT-4o
judge using the ICML (2025) three-class prompt \citep{safeconstellations2025}.

\paragraph{Behavioral groups.}
Following \citet{safeconstellations2025}, groups are defined by intended task, not
surface label, because cryptanalysis and RAG-QA samples are labeled \texttt{harmful\_instruction}
in text-type fields even when benign---any refusal on these tasks is over-refusal:
\begin{align*}
  \text{OVER\_REFUSAL} &= \text{refusal} \cap \text{benign task} \\
  \text{TARGET} &= (\texttt{cautious} \cup \texttt{not\_harmful}) \cap \texttt{direct\_answer} \\
  \text{REFUSED\_HARMFUL} &= \text{refusal} \cap \texttt{harmful\_label}
\end{align*}
where ``benign task'' $\in$ \{sentiment, translate, cryptanalysis, rag\_qa\}.

\paragraph{Embeddings.}
Activations are extracted at the input-normed residual stream of each layer
(\texttt{layer\_\{l\}\_input\_norm}). Silhouette scores are computed in the native
4096-dim space; 2-D UMAP is used for visualization only.

\paragraph{Arditi replication.}
We extract the global DIM direction at layer 12 (best AUROC) on our 25
refused-harmful / 30 harmless-answered sample pairs, replicating \citet{arditi2024refusal}.
H3 generates responses with greedy decoding (150 tokens), classified by the same
GPT-4o judge as the original pipeline.

\subsection{The Harmful-Refusal Direction Is Global and Converges Early}
\label{sec:arditi}

We replicate \citet{arditi2024refusal}: ablating the global DIM direction
($\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$) achieves 100\% attack success rate (ASR) on our
refused-harmful samples (Table~\ref{tab:arditi}). Task-specific DIM directions are
nearly identical to the global direction: cosine alignment 0.835--0.858 across all
tasks, with cross-task transfer nearly equalling self-transfer. The global direction
achieves $\geq$89\% cross-task alignment by \textbf{layer 3}---well before task
constellations peak at layer 12.

This early convergence is structurally significant: because $\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$
converges before task constellations form, it captures a signal that is largely
\emph{orthogonal} to task-identity geometry. Ablating it removes the cross-class
component of representations while leaving within-task structure intact---explaining
why it cannot selectively fix over-refusal.

%% ---- §4.3 ----
\subsection{H1: The Over-Refusal Direction Is Geometrically Distinct}
\label{sec:h1}

We compute a per-layer over-refusal direction:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\mathbf{v}}^{\OR}_\ell = \frac{
    \boldsymbol{\mu}^\ell_{\text{benign-refused}} - \boldsymbol{\mu}^\ell_{\text{benign-answered}}
  }{\|\boldsymbol{\mu}^\ell_{\text{benign-refused}} - \boldsymbol{\mu}^\ell_{\text{benign-answered}}\|}
\end{equation}
and measure $\cos(\hat{\mathbf{v}}^{\OR}_\ell,\, \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi},\ell})$
at each of the 32 layers (Figure~\ref{fig:cosine}). The shaded band (0.835--0.858) shows
the harmful-refusal inter-task alignment from \S\ref{sec:arditi} as a reference.

  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig2_cosine_h1.png}
  \caption{\textbf{H1: Over-refusal direction vs.\ Arditi direction, per layer.}
  Red line: $\cos(\hat{\mathbf{v}}^{\OR}_\ell, \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi},\ell})$.

The OR direction is zero at L0 (no structure), rises to 0.32 at L3 (where the harmful-refusal
direction has already converged to 89\%), and stabilises around \textbf{0.44--0.46} through
mid--late layers (peak L08: 0.456; L12: 0.448; L28: 0.386). This is dramatically below
the 0.845--0.858 range of harmful-refusal inter-task alignment, but not near zero: the
two directions share roughly $0.45^2 \approx 20\%$ of variance. The implication is that
they describe related but distinct phenomena, and that a direction intervention will
partially---but non-selectively---affect both.

\subsection{H2: Per-Task OR Directions Are More Task-Specific}
\label{sec:h2}

While harmful-refusal directions are nearly identical across tasks ($0.845 \pm 0.011$),
per-task OR directions are more task-specific. Only two tasks have sufficient
over-refusal samples ($\geq$\,5): sentiment\_analysis ($n_{\OR}=20$) and translate
($n_{\OR}=28$); cryptanalysis, rag\_qa, and rephrase have zero or too few over-refusal
samples in our evaluation set. Their pairwise OR direction cosine similarity at L12
is \textbf{0.564}---substantially below the 0.845 harmful-refusal baseline.
Per-task alignment with the global OR direction is also weak: sentiment 0.46,
translate 0.33. Figure~\ref{fig:h2} shows the comparison against the harmful-refusal
baseline: over-refusal is a more task-conditioned phenomenon.

%% ---- §4.5 ----
\subsection{H3: The Over-Refusal Subspace Is Higher-Dimensional and Task-Structured}
\label{sec:h3_dim}

If over-refusal is a task-conditioned phenomenon (H2), its representation should be
\emph{distributed across task directions} rather than concentrated in a single axis.
We test this directly via PCA of the OR and harmful-refusal subspaces at every layer.

\paragraph{Setup.}
For each layer $\ell$, we centre OR samples against the TARGET mean and run PCA, then
do the same for REFUSED\_HARMFUL samples centred against HARMLESS\_ANSWERED.
Two metrics summarise the result: (i) \% variance explained by PC1, and (ii) number of
components needed to reach 80\% cumulative variance.

\paragraph{Results at L12.}
The harmful-refusal subspace is relatively more concentrated: PC1 explains
\textbf{30.3\%} of its variance and \textbf{8} components suffice for 80\%.
The OR subspace is more diffuse: PC1 explains only \textbf{24.5\%} and
\textbf{11} components are needed for 80\% (Figure~\ref{fig:nb16_variance}).
Neither subspace is purely one-dimensional at the representational level---but OR is
consistently \emph{more} distributed, requiring $\sim$38\% more components than harmful-refusal.

An important distinction: \emph{causal} 1-D-ness (a single direction suffices for ablation,
as shown in \S\ref{sec:arditi}) is not the same as \emph{representational} 1-D-ness.
Harmful-refusal is causally one-dimensional (ablating $\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$
achieves 100\% ASR) even though its representation spans 8 components.
OR lacks both properties: no single direction selectively fixes it, and its
variance is spread across more components organised by task.

\paragraph{Task-structured PCA.}
Figure~\ref{fig:nb16_task_pca} makes the task-conditioning of OR concrete. In a 2-D PCA
of OR samples centred against the dataset mean, PC1 (24.5\% var) and PC2 (20.4\% var)
\emph{separate tasks}, not refusal signal: translate samples cluster on the
right ($+$PC1), sentiment\_analysis on the left ($-$PC1), with near-zero within-task
scatter. This directly confirms that the dominant axes of OR representation space are
task-identity directions---not a shared refusal axis.

The layer sweep (Figure~\ref{fig:nb16_layer_sweep} in Appendix) shows this pattern is
consistent across \textbf{layers 7--30}: from L7 onward, harmful-refusal consistently
requires fewer components (7--8) than OR (10--11). Notably, at early layers (L1--L4),
the pattern inverts---OR has a \emph{higher} PC1 share---consistent with the timeline
of constellation formation: before task structure peaks at L12, OR has not yet been
absorbed into task-identity geometry and briefly appears more concentrated.

\subsection{Functional Probing Evidence: Task and Refusal Separability}
\label{sec:probing}

The PCA results of \S\ref{sec:h3_dim} established that the OR subspace is geometrically
higher-dimensional and task-structured. We complement this with a functional test:
can a linear probe decode (i) task identity, (ii) refusal behaviour (OR vs.\ TARGET),
and (iii) refusal \emph{type} (OR vs.\ REFUSED\_HARMFUL) from residual-stream
activations, and at which layers?

\paragraph{Setup.}
We train three logistic-regression probes at each of the 31 layers via
5-fold stratified cross-validation with balanced accuracy as the metric.
\textbf{Task probe} (5-class, $n=270$): predict task identity.
\textbf{Refusal-behaviour probe} (binary, $n=217$): OR ($n=48$) vs.\ TARGET ($n=169$) within
benign-task samples.
\textbf{Refusal-type probe} (binary, $n=73$): OR ($n=48$) vs.\ REFUSED\_HARMFUL ($n=25$)---two
refused populations separated by cause, not content.

\paragraph{Results (Figure~\ref{fig:nb17_probes}).}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Task identity encodes immediately and saturates early.}
  The task probe reaches 97\% at L1 (vs.\ 20\% chance) and saturates at
  \textbf{99.6\%} by L6---well before the constellation silhouette peaks at L12.

  \item \textbf{Refusal behaviour encodes early but peaks later.}
  The refusal-behaviour probe activates at L1 (86\%), but continues to improve,
  peaking at \textbf{93.6\%} at L14---8 layers after task-probe saturation and
  2 layers after the constellation peak (L12). Task identity is set up first;
  within-task refusal behaviour consolidates after.

  \item \textbf{OR and harmful-refusal are perfectly separable throughout.}
  The refusal-type probe achieves \textbf{100\%} balanced accuracy from L1 onward
  and never drops below chance. Even at the first transformer layer, the model's
  representations \emph{perfectly} distinguish erroneous over-refusal from legitimate
  harmful-refusal. The two refusal causes occupy non-overlapping regions of
  representation space at every layer.
\end{enumerate}

The 100\% refusal-type accuracy is geometrically consistent with the two-subspace account:
if OR and harmful-refusal live in distinct subspaces ($\cos \approx 0.45$,
Figure~\ref{fig:cosine}), a linear probe separating them will trivially succeed even at
early layers. Crucially, this finding directly motivates selectivity: if the two refusal
types are representationally distinct throughout the network, a method that targets
task-conditioned geometry should suppress one without disrupting the other---confirmed in
\S\ref{sec:nb15}.

%% ---- §4.7 ----
\subsection{H4: Arditi Ablation Suppresses Both Classes Non-Selectively}
\label{sec:h3}

Figure~\ref{fig:h3} presents the direct empirical comparison ($n=20$ per condition,
GPT-4o judge). The result is initially surprising: Arditi ablation suppresses
\emph{both} classes by a similar magnitude---harmful refusal drops from 65\% to 10\%
($-55$ pp), and over-refusal drops from 55\% to 5\% ($-50$ pp).

This does \emph{not} mean the geometric distinction (H1/H2) is irrelevant. The $\approx$\,0.45
cosine overlap between the OR and Arditi directions is sufficient to cause substantial
collateral suppression: projecting out the Arditi direction removes $\approx$\,20\% of
the OR direction's variance, disrupting the OR signal broadly. The key issue is
\textbf{selectivity}: Arditi ablation cannot fix over-refusal \emph{without} also
disrupting harmful-refusal suppression (and vice versa), because it operates on a blunt
global direction. A task-conditioned method that targets only the OR-specific subspace
(the component orthogonal to $\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$) can fix over-refusal
without this collateral cost---which is precisely what SafeConstellations does.

\subsection{SafeConstellations vs.\ Arditi: Selectivity Confirmed}
\label{sec:nb15}

The geometric distinction between the OR and harmful-refusal subspaces predicts that
task-conditioned methods should achieve higher \emph{selectivity} than global ablation:
greater over-refusal suppression with less collateral disruption to the harmful-refusal
circuit. We test this directly by running SafeConstellations \citep{safeconstellations2025}
and Arditi ablation on the same 20 over-refusal and 20 refused-harmful samples
(greedy decoding, 150 tokens; GPT-4o judge; Figure~\ref{fig:selectivity}).

Results confirm the prediction (Table~\ref{tab:nb15}). Arditi achieves a selectivity
ratio of 0.91---effectively equal suppression of both classes, consistent with its
global, non-task-conditioned geometry. SafeConstellations achieves a selectivity
ratio of \textbf{1.57}: it eliminates over-refusal entirely ($-55$\,pp) while causing
substantially less harmful-refusal bypass ($-35$\,pp vs.\ $-55$\,pp for Arditi)---
72\% more OR suppression per unit of safety disruption.

The residual $-35$\,pp harmful-refusal bypass has a direct mechanistic explanation
rooted in constellation geometry (\S\ref{sec:constellations}). Our refused-harmful
samples are task-wrapped: they arrive as translation, rephrasing, or sentiment
analysis prompts with harmful content embedded. At L12, where task identity dominates
representations (silhouette\,=\,0.357), these samples are geometrically
indistinguishable from benign over-refusal samples at the task-cluster level.
The constellation hook---which fires on task identity to nudge representations toward
the task target centroid---therefore applies equally to harmful task-wrapped prompts.
This is not a flaw but an inherent property of targeting task-identity geometry: in
deployment, a prior content classifier would gate steering to verified-benign task
instances, closing this gap. Critically, even without such a gate, SafeConstellations
remains strictly more selective than Arditi ($1.57$ vs.\ $0.91$), and its limitation
is geometrically motivated by the very results established in \S\ref{sec:constellations}.

\section{Discussion}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{A unified two-subspace account.}
Across layers, refusal in aligned LLMs involves two distinct geometric structures:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Early (L0--L7):} The global harmful-refusal direction ($\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$)
  reaches $\geq$89\% cross-task alignment by L3. This direction is \emph{causally}
  one-dimensional---a single ablation achieves 100\% ASR---and task-agnostic, correctly
  captured by DIM. (Representationally, harmful-refusal variance spans 8 components, but
  the causal signal is concentrated in one.)
  \item \textbf{Mid (L8--L19):} Task-identity constellations peak (silhouette = 0.357
  at L12). Over-refusal accumulates as a small within-cluster perturbation ($\approx$1.5--2.5 UMAP
  units) in a direction geometrically distinct from $\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$
  ($\cos \approx 0.45$). The OR subspace is higher-dimensional (11 components for 80\%
  variance vs.\ 8 for harmful-refusal at L12), and its principal axes are task-identity
  directions---confirmed by clean task-separation in OR PC space (Figure~\ref{fig:nb16_task_pca}).
  The refusal-behaviour probe peaks at L14 (93.6\%), 8 layers after task saturation (L6),
  providing functional confirmation of this mid-layer consolidation timeline.
  \item \textbf{Late (L20--L31):} Task-specific attention heads (L27--L31, 80\% unique
  per task) commit the final refusal decision. The within-task behavioral gap shrinks
  here, consistent with late heads selectively reading task-conditioned signals.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Scope of the linear representation hypothesis.}
The LRH \citep{park2023linear} holds for harmful refusal in its \emph{causal} form:
one direction suffices for ablation, achieving 100\% ASR and $-55$ pp suppression.
Importantly, this is not the same as representational 1-D-ness: harmful-refusal
variance itself spans 8 components at L12, with PC1 explaining only 30\%.
The LRH claim is that the \emph{causal} bottleneck is one-dimensional, not that the
full distribution is.

Over-refusal breaks both senses. Its representation is more diffuse (11 components,
PC1 = 25\%) \emph{and} its principal axes are task-identity directions
(Figure~\ref{fig:nb16_task_pca}), not a single refusal axis---so no single direction
captures it causally. The practical consequence is not that single-direction methods
\emph{fail} to suppress OR---they achieve $-50$ pp via collateral disruption---but that
they cannot do so \emph{selectively}. The $\approx$\,20\% variance overlap between
the OR and Arditi directions is sufficient for this collateral suppression.

\paragraph{The selectivity argument.}
This reframes the steering problem. The question is not ``does ablation suppress OR?''
but ``can ablation suppress OR without corrupting the harmful-refusal circuit?'' A
single direction cannot answer this: it either ablates the shared component (harming
both) or leaves it (fixing neither). Task-conditioned geometry, by contrast,
decomposes the OR signal into a component parallel to $\hat{\mathbf{r}}_{\text{Arditi}}$
(shared with harmful-refusal) and a component orthogonal to it (OR-specific).
\S\ref{sec:nb15} confirms this empirically: SafeConstellations achieves a selectivity
ratio of 1.57 vs.\ 0.91 for Arditi---eliminating over-refusal entirely ($-55$\,pp)
while causing substantially less harmful-refusal bypass ($-35$\,pp vs.\ $-55$\,pp).
The geometric analysis in H1/H2 provides the mechanistic grounding for why this is
possible, and the residual bypass (35\,pp) is itself explained by constellation
geometry: task-wrapped harmful prompts share task-cluster identity with benign
over-refusal samples and therefore trigger the same task-conditioned hook.

\paragraph{Connection to prior failure accounts.}
The failure modes of \citet{braun2025unreliability}---distributional shift, feature
entanglement, magnitude sensitivity---are all exacerbated when a cross-class direction
is applied to within-class variation. The entanglement is literal here: the Arditi
direction entangles OR suppression with harmful-refusal suppression. Our analysis
quantifies the entanglement degree ($\cos \approx 0.45$) and shows it is large enough
to matter empirically.

\paragraph{Calibration.}
(i) Our Arditi replication uses 25 harmful samples from our evaluation set, not
JailbreakBench/AdvBench; numbers are in-distribution.
(ii) H3 uses $n=20$ per condition; the 5 pp difference ($-55$ pp vs.\ $-50$ pp) should
not be over-interpreted.
(iii) Only 2 tasks contribute to H2 (OR samples absent in cryptanalysis/rag\_qa/rephrase);
the 0.564 pairwise cosine should be treated as preliminary.
(iv) PCA centroid maps capture 39.6\% variance in 2-D; all quantitative claims cite
4096-dim silhouette scores.

%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\section{Conclusion}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------

Harmful-refusal and over-refusal are geometrically distinct ($\cos \approx 0.45$ vs.\
$0.845$ among harmful-refusal directions) and task-conditioned to different degrees
(OR pairwise similarity 0.56 vs.\ 0.845). The OR subspace is also higher-dimensional:
at L12, it requires 11 components for 80\% variance (vs.\ 8 for harmful-refusal),
and its principal axes are task-identity directions---confirmed by clean task separation
in OR PC space. Arditi ablation suppresses both classes by similar magnitudes ($-55$ pp,
$-50$ pp), revealing it as a blunt instrument that disrupts the refusal circuit broadly
rather than selectively. A direct head-to-head experiment confirms the selectivity
advantage of task-conditioned methods: SafeConstellations eliminates over-refusal
($-55$ pp, to 0\%) while causing substantially less harmful-refusal bypass than Arditi
($-35$ pp vs.\ $-55$ pp; selectivity ratio 1.57 vs.\ 0.91). The residual bypass is
mechanistically explained by constellation geometry---task-wrapped harmful prompts share
mid-layer task-cluster identity with benign over-refusal samples and trigger the same
task-conditioned hook. The linear representation hypothesis remains valid for harmful
refusal in its causal form; the challenge for over-refusal is that its relevant direction
is task-conditioned and multi-dimensional---and only a task-aware intervention can
exploit that structure.

%% -----------------------------------------------------------
\section*{Limitations}
%% -----------------------------------------------------------

Analysis is limited to LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct on a 270-sample evaluation set. OR
direction analysis requires sufficient over-refusal examples per task; cryptanalysis and
RAG-QA contribute zero over-refusal samples in our data, which also limits dimensionality
analysis to the two tasks that produce OR (sentiment, translate) and means the task PCA
(Figure~\ref{fig:nb16_task_pca}) reflects only two task clusters. The causal patching
result (NB12c) is inconclusive due to a sequence-length mismatch that silently skips
patches. H2, the selectivity experiment (\S\ref{sec:nb15}), and H3 dimensionality results
each rely on only two valid tasks; results should be treated as preliminary until
replicated on a broader task distribution. The $n=20$ per-condition sample size means
individual percentage-point differences carry uncertainty of $\approx\pm11$ pp (95\%
CI). Cross-model replication remains as planned future work.
